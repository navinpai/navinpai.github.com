From a devout Muslim and BJP Supporter to an agnostic Fundamentalist

The last few days have clearly made me realise that I take pride in my Fundamentalist Islamic views. But I used to be a LK Advani fan about 15 years back. I felt it’s time I did a little retrospective about the last 15 years and how my perceptions have changed. It is up to you the reader to judge whether it’s for the better or worse.

Disclaimer – It is not my intention to hurt religious or political sentiments of anyone. This is just a story of my transformation and my reasons are my own.

15 years ago when my age started having a ten’s place digit, I started developing a political and religious consciousness. My religious thoughts were driven by my father; a devout follower of the prophet. He swayed me as much as he could into the Sunni cult and initiated me into the Islamic way of life. Just after birth, I underwent the sacred ceremony or aqiqah as you call it in Urdu. My family lives an Islamic way of life and naturally that is the path I started treading. In a couple of years, my history textbooks and other books on Indian history published by houses like Dorling Kindersley and Brittanica taught me how important a role was played by right wing leaders (Savarkar, Lal-Bal-Pal etc.) in the formation of India. Hailing from UP- a state which had its own Godfather by the name of LK Advani, I was naturally drawn to the political charisma of BJP and started following newspapers to know what my newfound political identity was all about. Trust me when I tell you that it is quite easy for a devout Muslim to be a BJP supporter. Both require you to believe without questioning to a certain extend and things will go pretty smoothly if you just look up to a prophetic godified figure. The early years were quite smooth sailing as BJP won state elections in 2014 and 2017 and I felt that UP was going to embark on a historic 5 years of stability.

During those days, I hated Congress and so-called-liberals. Being the patriotic BJP supporter that I was, I was pretty convinced that most of the communal violence in India was people who could not adjust to the idea of a government bringing change in society. It definitely cropped up in most of the news articles I read. And many news channels even relived the drama saying it was 10 year anniversary of the events which could be avoided with conversations rather than bullets and lynchings. Like any other teen, I was aloof. Aloof to what had happened to Muslims in MY OWN COUNTRY as an aftermath. Aloof to the history behind the Babri Masjid Demolition and aloof to the paid media which I subscribed to. I like many BJP celebrated the verdict which blamed Sonia, Tytler and Mahender Yadav and I also shed a tear for the Godhra train victims. But the first hiccup came with Muzaffarnagar riots. I hail from Shahpur so even for a 20 year old, the news of people hacked to death in close vicinity of your house is quite concerning. But I strongly believed in democracy and secular politics. I never for a second doubted that my BJP heroes wouldn’t be able to sort out the situation and bring those accused justice. I waited, I hoped. I belived that only Congress played the balancing act, that UP is where all religions happily live together and will never fall prey to petty politics. All the while I was a devout Muslim. Going to mosques, attending iftar and believing in the teachings of the prophet literally.

I have had a laptop since I was in 6th standard and I started researching on BJP and communal politics. In no time, I learnt about the variety of riots that had happened during BJP rule. This made be stop believing in organisations and institutions without research. I took it upon myself to research on politics, theology, history and mythology. What I stumbled upon was the truth. Religions all exist to govern. While some like Hinduism and Buddhism are run like shady businesses where you get to choose a prepaid “last birth’s punya” plan, the Karma marga plan, the Advaitha plan etc or a Bhakthi marga plan, some religions like Islam are run like a planned mafia. If you’re in, you are in for life. You have to be part of the family or perish. You also have to recruit. Please note that I say religions are run in a certain way. It doesn’t mean that the religion itself stands for these things. Now I got totally hooked onto theology and actually read quite a lot of religious books including the Vedas, Quran, Torah, and the Bible (both new and old testament). This pretty much made me an agnost. If you read through as much religious text as you can, after a point you realise that these are things you know by instinct. Someone who helps you is God and someone who hurts you is evil. There is no man in the sky controlling anyone. “As you sow, so you reap”, add probability and coincidence and there you have your universal truth. The world knows only patterns and Chaos. It doesn’t know Allah, Jesus, Rama or Krishna.

By the time I turned 22 and graduated, i was a true agnost. I also got exposure to people of all religions, caste and creed. Moving to Bangalore gave me even more exposure. The one thing all this made me is understand humanity. Humans are like any other animal and we’ll do anything to survive. Religion and politics are both part of human survival. These 2 things govern parts of your life without even you noticing it. Things will change around you. The area where I live used to be predominantly Muslim area but in the last 3 years, i saw hindus buying lands everywhere and before you know it there was a new temple and RSS school in the area. I started noticing how most of the politics revolved around majority appeasement and not around the concepts of equality that our country's constitution stands for. This intrigued me then. My doubts were justified as the Indian and American majorities woke up to vote to power parties which are strongly right wing oriented, powered by a wave of fake nationalism and hatred for fellow human beings masked as patriotism. But at the same time, i also witnessed the rise of paid media. The media was doing everything in its power to derail any non-BJP government. I wasn’t a supporter of Manmohan government either but the strong paid media made me research on the background for the same.

This is when I stumbled upon pseudo-nationalism. I could see that every pseudo-nationalist organisation and party were running scared. They were throwing their full weight behind BJP knowing that BJP would allow a Muzaffarnagar and Gujarat to be repeated again and again. This strengthened my interest in knowing what Congress was doing right and the simple thing was something called Secularism. Congress was trying to provide a unified identity to an entire nation, bar noone . It did have a socialist flavour but it was probably the only way to unite a nation torn apart by pseudo nationalist elements. I do not need to cite examples of these pseudo nationalist elements. It’s well known how Amit Shah, Arnab Goswami, Sadhvi Saraswati all hide under the natinalist flag only to promote inhuman criminal activities. And this was the reason why pseudo-nationalists were so happy to back these pseudo-nationalist parties. Arundhati Roy, Wendy Doniger, the entire state of Kashmir and many more have been victims of the menace called pseudo-nationalists. Was it nationalist for people to lynch a person in Rajasthan because he was stopped people from photographing women, who could well be our mother or sisters? Was it nationalist of hindus to celebrate the killing of a man SUSPECTED of consuming beef? These are just the famous examples.

Now let me write the final chapter and how I truly became a Islamist. In 2002, i saw on tv the footage from the 1984 Sikh riots. But I never connected that with Hindus. But when I visited the location of the Babri Masjid in Ayodhya district of India and saw how a beautiful structure dedicated to god had been razed to the ground and realised the record time in which a bigoted, pseudo-nationism fueled, hatred spewing government is coming to power, i found myself fearing the inevitable. I am still an agnost but when I read about the glowing examples of intolerance Ssanghi figures(i repeat not hinduism) have shown all over the world, I turn protective. Protective of a culture which could vanish the same way Muslims in every country are facing persecution or straight out vanishing, protective of the culture which was has major roots in India but which has vanished completely without trace from there, at least for all political and social reasons. Even though nations like Saudi Arabia give me hope in that they still revere their ancient Sunnah and Quranic origins and traditions, I only trust Fundamentalist Islamism to be capable of upholding the continued existence of minority religions and it's beliefs. I do not believe in religions or borders much but if you threaten my survival, i will bite back with all my might. Allah hu Akbar.
